Choose Your Ascent

Púlpito del Diablo

Distance: 17.2 km (Round-trip) | Max Elevation: 4,800 m

Crosses the Lagunillas Valley, leading to the massive, square rock structure sitting on the edge of the Pan de Azúcar glacier.

Laguna Grande

Distance: 15.8 km (Round-trip) | Max Elevation: 4,600 m

A demanding trail offering panoramic views of the southern Sierra Nevada and high-altitude glacial lakes. Optional: +2.5 km to view the Concavo glacier.

Ritacuba Blanco

Distance: 15.3 km (Round-trip) | Max Elevation: 4,970 m

A direct alpine approach to the base of Ritacuba Blanco (5,330 m), the highest summit in Colombia's Eastern Andes.

Logistics & Park Protocols

Official Park Fees Breakdown

Mandatory High-Altitude Insurance: $13,700 COP per day.

Foreigner Park Entry Permit: $102,500 COP (Valid for a 4-day window).

Private 4x4 Overland Transport: 1-hour drive each way from the village to the trailheads.

The Mandatory Briefing Protocol

To validate your physical permit, National Parks requires a mandatory 15-minute virtual induction briefing (held daily at 8:00 AM and 4:00 PM). I will manage your registration queue, but you must attend this session live via the connection link we will share over WhatsApp.

Boot Policy

Must use broken-in trekking boots. Do not wear brand-new or heavily worn-out footwear; the abrasive moraine rock will damage weak soles.
